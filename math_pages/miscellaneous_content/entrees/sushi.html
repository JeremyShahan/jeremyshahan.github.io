Sushi Recipe

Image

Fit

Description

Sushi (すし, 寿司, 鮨, 鮓) is a Japanese dish of prepared vinegared rice (鮨飯, sushi-meshi), usually with some sugar and salt, plus a variety of ingredients (ねた, neta), such as vegetables, meat, or most commonly, seafood, which may be raw or cooked. There are many styles of sushi, and its presentation varies widely, but the one key ingredient is "sushi rice", also referred to as shari (しゃり), or sumeshi.

Ingredients

2 cups of uncooked white rice 3 cups of water 1/2 cup of rice vinegar
1 tablespoon of vegetable oil 1/4 cup of white sugar 1 teaspoon of salt

Tools

full cup 1/4 cup full tablespoon full teaspoon
strainer medium pot small pan solid spatula

Instructions

Sushi Rice

  • Put the 2 cups of rice in a strainer and rinse the rice until the water that runs through is clear.
  • Add rice and 3 cups of water to medium pot and cook over medium heat and bring to a boil.
  • Once boiling, reduce temperature to a low heat and then cover the pot.
  • Cook until the rice is tender and all the water is absorbed; usually about 20 minutes.
  • Remove the pot from heat, and let it cool a little.

Vineagar Mixture

  • Add 1/2 cup of rice vineagar, 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, 1/4 cup white sugar, and 1 teaspoon of salt to the small pan.
  • Cook over medium heat until the sugar has been disolved.
  • Stir the mixture into the cooked rice. Keep stirring until the the rice absorbs the vinegar mixture and appears dry.
