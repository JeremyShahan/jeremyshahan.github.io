Shrimp Coconut Curry

Description

Ingredients

3 tablespoons of vegetable oil 1/2 teaspoons of black mustard seeds 8 fresh curry leaves
1 large red onion (chopped) 1 teaspoon of ginger (grated) 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
1 teaspoon of sambar masala powder or curry powder 1/2 teaspooons of tumeric salt
14 ounces of coconut water or coconut milk 12 colossal shrimp (shelled with tails on) rice

Tools

2 medium pans medium bowl solid spatula full tablespoon
full teaspoon 1/4 teaspoon

Instructions

Curry

  • In a medium pan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
  • Add 1/2 a teaspoon of the mustard seeds and as soon as the seeds begin to sputter, add the curry leaves, onions, and ginger.
  • Saute until the onions are golden brown, usually around 5 to 7 minutes.
  • Reduce heat to low and add the chili powder, sambar masala, a 1/4 teaspoon of tumeric, and some salt. Saute for 1 minute.
  • Add the coconut water (or milk) and simmer until the mixture is slightly thickened and has turned a deeper shade of gold, about 5 minutes.

Shrimp

  • Combine 1/4 teaspoons of tumeric and some salt in a medium bowl.
  • Pat dry the shrimp then add them into the bowl and toss to coat.
  • Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a medium pan over medium heat.
  • Add the shrimp and cook, tossing occasionally, until pink, about 4 to 6 minutes.
  • Add the shrimp to the curry and serve over rice.

Coconut Sambol

Ingredients

1 and 1/2 cups of grated coconut meat juice of 1 lime 1 green chili (chopped roughly)
1 tablespoon coriander (chopped) 3 tablespoons onion (chopped) 1 teaspoon of kashmiri chili or paprika
1/2 teaspoon of salt

Tools

mortar and pestle medium bowl 1/2 cup full tablespoon
full teaspoon 1/2 teaspoon

Instructions

Topping

  • Add 6 egg whites into clean medium bowl and whisk until slightly frothly.
  • Add in 12 tablespoons of sugar and continue to whisk until it turns creamy. This should take about 10 - 15 minutes for one egg white up to 40 minutes for 6 egg whites.
  • Add in 6 teaspoons of vanilla extract and continue whisking until mixture is firm.

Things to Try for Next Time

  • Avoid mixing in cinnamon and instead perhaps sprinkle some on at the end.
  • Add slightly less nutmeg. Perhaps only 1 teaspoon of nutmeg.
  • My stove in BR is funky; try the bottom right burner set to 2 and 1/2 at most! Perhaps something slightly cooler is even better, like at 2 and 1/4.
  • Also, if cooking for longer than 15 minutes, try turning down the temperature slightly after 15 minutes. Probably try this much later. It was too thick anyway when heated for longer.
