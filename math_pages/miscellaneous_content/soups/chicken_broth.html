Chicken Broth Recipe

Image

Fit

Description

Chicken broth is a flavorful, savory liquid made by simmering chicken meat (and sometimes bones) along with vegetables, herbs, and spices, and is often used as a base for soups, stews, and sauces.

Ingredients

3 pounds of chicken (offal, rib cage, torso) 12 cups of water 1 medium onion
2 medium carrots 2 sticks of celery 2 garlic cloves
1 teaspoon of salt 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
10 whole peppercorns 1 bay leaf

Tools

large pot solid spatula strainer knife
full teaspoon 1/2 teaspoon full cup

Instructions

  • In the large pot, add the 3 pounds of chicken and the 12 cups of water. (Usually, the chicken I get comes from the leftovers from a whole chicken from the store)
  • Mince the garlic cloves and add them to the pot.
  • Chop up the vegetables so that the pieces are small, but substantially larger than the peppercorns and then add to the pot. This will make the removal of the peppercorns easier later on.
  • Next, add in the remaining ingredients and heat up the pot until simmering.
  • Let the pot simmer, not boil, for several hours. I don't recommend frequently skimming the top off the broth like other recipes suggest as this may remove many of the spices that you have added.
  • Once you are done simmering the broth, you can run the liquid through a strainer to catch all the ingredients.
  • If you intend to use the broth for soup, you can add back in the remaining ingredients while removing the peppercorns.
  • Otherwise, if you are saving the broth for something else, you can remove all the ingredients and only save the broth.
