Eggnog Recipe

Image

Fit

Description

Eggnog is a rich, creamy, and chilled dairy-based drink that's typically made with milk or cream, sugar, egg yolks, and whipped egg whites. The whipped egg whites give eggnog its frothy texture and its name. Eggnog is often served cold and has a custard-like color and texture. It can also have a slightly spiced flavor from nutmeg and cinnamon.

Ingredients

3 cups whole milk 6 eggs 1 cup sugar
2 teaspoons of vanilla extract 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoons of ground cloves
1 tablespoon and 2 teaspoons of ginger powder 1 and 1/2 teaspooons of ground nutmeg 1 and 1/2 cups of heavy whipping cream
1/4 cup of rum (optional)

Tools

whisk medium pot medium bowl small bowl
solid spatula strainer full teaspoon 1/4 teaspoon
full tablespoon full cup 1/2 cup 1/4 cup (optional)

Instructions

Drink

  • Measure out 3 cups of whole milk and add it to medium pot.
  • Heat up the milk to a steam, but not to a simmer.
  • Separate the egg yolks and place them into a medium bowl.
  • Whisk yolks until slightly lighter in color, and while whisking, gradually add 1 cup of sugar to the mixture.
  • When milk is hot and steamy, slowly add the milk into bowl, while whisking the eggs.
  • Add mixture back into the pot, and add in 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract.
  • Cook on low heat for 10 - 15 minutes or until thickened. Keep the temperature of the mixture close to, but below, 175 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • The longer it is being cooked, the thicker the mixture will be. Thicker mixtures are better for mixing in the gound cinnamon.
  • Pour contents into clean medium bowl through strainer.

Spices

  • In a small bowl add:
    • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves
    • 1 tablespoon + 2 teaspoons of ginger powder
    • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons of ground nutmeg.

Drink

  • While stirring the yolk mixture, slowly add in the spices.
  • Add in 1/4 cup of rum and mix (optional).
  • Add in 1 and 1/2 cups of heavy whipping cream.
  • Put mixture in ice water bath while mixing.
  • Store eggnog in a bowl or some glasses and let it cool in the fridge for at least two hours.

Eggnog Topping

Ingredients

6 eggs whites 12 tablespoons of sugar 6 teaspoons vanila extract

Tools

whisk medium bowl measuring spoons/cups

Instructions

Topping

  • Add 6 egg whites into clean medium bowl and whisk until slightly frothly.
  • Add in 12 tablespoons of sugar and continue to whisk until it turns creamy. This should take about 10 - 15 minutes for one egg white up to 40 minutes for 6 egg whites.
  • Add in 6 teaspoons of vanilla extract and continue whisking until mixture is firm.

Things to Try for Next Time

  • Avoid mixing in cinnamon and instead perhaps sprinkle some on at the end.
  • Add slightly less nutmeg. Perhaps only 1 teaspoon of nutmeg.
  • My stove in BR is funky; try the bottom right burner set to 2 and 1/2 at most! Perhaps something slightly cooler is even better, like at 2 and 1/4.
  • Also, if cooking for longer than 15 minutes, try turning down the temperature slightly after 15 minutes. Probably try this much later. It was too thick anyway when heated for longer.
